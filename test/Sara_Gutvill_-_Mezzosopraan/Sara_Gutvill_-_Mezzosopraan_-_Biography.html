Sára Gutvill mezzo soprane

Sará was was born in Budapest, Hungary. She studied German literature at the University of Vienna before she moved to the Netherlands where she currently lives. In Vienna Sára started to perform Jewish vocal music with different ensembles.

In Amsterdam she studied with Pierre Mak at the Conservatorium van Amsterdam and finished her studies in 2007.

She has been working with many distinguished teachers including Magda Nádor, Paula de Wit, Margreet Honig, Valérie Guillorit, Rudolf Jansen, Maarten Koningsberger, Udo Reinemann and Claron McFadden.

Sára has a broad repertoire. She loves to sing lied and performs regularly with pianist Harimada Kusuma. Sára’s love for Jewish Music remained unchanged and she keeps performing Yiddish and Ladino songs among others with the great Armenian accordeonist David Yengibarjan.

She often sings Baroque music with her ensemble the Baroque Chamber Players of Amsterdam.

In February 2008 Sára performed Ottorino Respighi’s Il Tramonto with the Párkányi Quartet in the Concertgebouw in Amsterdam. During the summer of 2008 Sára performed in Purcells Dido and Aneas in a project with Claron McFadden singing the role of Second Woman.
